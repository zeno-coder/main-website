Advanced Diagnostics

What to Expect During Your Eye Test

Our comprehensive eye examination takes 45–60 minutes and covers every aspect of your vision and eye health.

01

Visual Acuity Test

We measure how clearly you see at various distances using standard letter charts and digital projectors. This forms the baseline for your prescription.

02

Refraction Assessment

Using a phoropter or autorefractor, we determine the exact lens prescription needed to correct nearsightedness, farsightedness, or astigmatism.

03

Eye Pressure (Tonometry)

A painless air-puff or contact tonometry test measures intraocular pressure — a key indicator for glaucoma risk.

04

Retinal Examination

Digital fundus photography and OCT scanning give us a detailed view of your retina, optic nerve, and blood vessels to detect early pathology.

05

Colour Vision & Depth Perception

Ishihara colour plates and stereopsis tests assess colour deficiency and binocular vision function.

06

Report & Prescription

We review all findings with you, provide a written report, prescribe corrective lenses if needed, and plan any follow-up care.

Preventive Care

Why Regular Eye Checkups Matter

Many serious eye conditions — glaucoma, diabetic retinopathy, macular degeneration — have no symptoms in their early stages. Routine checkups are your first line of defence.

0–5 yrs
Infant & Toddler Screening At 6 months, 3 years, and before starting school
6–18 yrs
School-Age Children Every 1–2 years or as recommended
19–40 yrs
Adults (Low Risk) Every 2 years; annually if wearing correction
41–60 yrs
Middle-Aged Adults Annually — presbyopia and glaucoma screening
61+ yrs
Seniors Every 6–12 months for comprehensive eye health monitoring

Prevention First

Early detection saves sight. 80% of vision loss is avoidable with timely care.
